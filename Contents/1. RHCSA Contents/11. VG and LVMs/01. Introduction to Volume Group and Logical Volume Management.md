## **Problem**

In the previous sections, we have learned how to divide a disk to partitions and mount them to directories to use. As I mentioned there, there was a problem; when the partition is full, we cannot delete some data or increase the partition size. That causes a lot of problems. We have a temporary solution for that which is `growpart` command. However, that cannot be permanent. 

---

## **Solution**

To solve that problem, we have a powerfull logic which is **Volume Group and Logical Volume Management.** Here we basically create a pool (Volume Group) and give all [[01. Introduction to Disk|disk]]/[[02. Partitioning and Its Types|partition]] space to it. Then instead of mounting our partitions directly to directories, we create LVM-s from this pool which are resizable. So, when free space of any LVM runs out of, we can add a new space from the pool easily.
