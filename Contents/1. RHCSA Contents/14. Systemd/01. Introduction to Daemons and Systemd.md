## **What is Daemon?**

A **daemon** is a background process that runs silently without direct user interaction. It starts (usually at boot) and just keeps running — waiting to do its job when needed. On Linux/Unix, daemon processes typically end with `d` — `sshd`, `httpd`, `cron`**d**.

Examples: a web server waiting for requests, a print spooler waiting for print jobs.

---

## **What is Systemd?**

**systemd** is the "init system" on most modern [[01. Introduction to Linux|Linux]] distros. It's the **first [[01. Introduction to Process|process]] that starts** when Linux boots (PID 1), and it's responsible for:

- Starting and stopping all other daemons/services
- Managing the boot sequence
- Handling dependencies (e.g., "start the database _before_ the web server")
- Logging (via `journald`)

---

## **Relation between Daemon and Systemd**

systemd **manages** daemons.

- Daemons are the _what_ — the background processes doing actual work
- systemd is the _who controls them_ — starts, stops, monitors, and restarts them

Think of it like a **manager (systemd) and workers (daemons)**. The workers do the jobs, the manager decides when they start, stops them if needed, and restarts them if they crash.
