## **What is LV?**

**LV** stands for **Logical Volume** ŌĆö it's the partition-like slice you carve out from a [[03. Volume Group|Volume Group]].

The big difference from a regular [[02. Partitioning and Its Types|partition]] is that you can **resize it later** easily, as long as the VG has free space.

---

## **Commands**

##### Creating LV

We have a command to create LV:

```bash
lvcreate -n <NAME> -L <SIZE> <VG_NAME>
lvcreate -n <NAME> -l <SIZE> <VG_NAME>
```

Both specify the size of the LV, but in different units:
- `-L` takes a size in human-readable units like `500M`, `2G`, `1T`.

- `-l` takes a number of **[[04. Physical Extend|PEs]]**. So `-l 100` means "give me 100 Physical Extents." You can also use `-l 100%FREE` to consume all remaining free space in the VG.

In practice, `-L` is easier and more intuitive for everyday use.

##### Display LV

We have a command `lvs` to show all LVs. For detailed information we use:

```bash
lvdisplay #About all lvs
lvdisplay /dev/vg01/lv01 #For specific lv
```
